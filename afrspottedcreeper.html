African Spotted Creeper

Salpornis salvadori

The African spotted creeper (Salpornis salvadori) is a small, slender bird with a long, downcurved bill. It is typically about 13-14 cm in length and weighs around 9-11 grams. The male and female have similar plumage, with a mostly brown body and a distinctive white stripe over the eye. The wings and tail are tipped with white, and the underparts are pale with dark streaking

It is found in sub-Saharan Africa in open deciduous forest and mango groves. It does not migrate other than local movements.

Behaviour & Diet

The African spotted creeper feeds on insects and other small invertebrates, which it finds by searching through bark crevices and other small spaces on tree trunks and branches. It has a distinctive, undulating flight and tends to fly only short distances at a time. During the breeding season, the African spotted creeper builds a small, cup-shaped nest in a tree or bush. The female lays a clutch of 2-4 eggs, which are incubated for about two weeks. Both parents help to feed the chicks until they fledge. In general, the African spotted creeper is a timid and elusive bird that is more often heard than seen. It has a soft, warbling song that is often heard in the early morning and late afternoon.
